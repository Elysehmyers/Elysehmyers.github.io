Who We Are

The Caribbean-American Family Network Mission Statement

We develop a cohesive, sustainable, faith-based Caribbean-American community by sponsoring activities which provide support, resources, and opportunities to expand and enhance the Caribbean-American experience.

Our Start

CAFN was conceived and founded in 1995 by Hasana Pennant-Sisco and Rodney Sisco. Hasana was born and raised in Kingston, Jamaica while her husband, Rodney Sisco, was born and raised in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The Sisco’s held their first CAFN Picnic in 1995 at the Starved Rock State Park in Peru, Illinois as an effort to remain connected to their Caribbean roots and recognize their bi-cultural heritage.

Our Support

Hasana and Rodney were joined by their cousins Sandra Kelly-Harris (Jamaica) and Kirk Harris (US) along with 35 other attendees. Four countries were faithfully represented and celebrated that day: Jamaica, Trinidad, Guyana, and the United States. From that day onward, CAFN has continued to gain momentum and support families across the world.

Our Growth

By 2000, CAFN's annual picnics had risen from 35 attendees to well over 250! The increasing support and explosive growth for these hosted events led to the calling of CAFN’s first Board in January, 2000. By July of the same year, CAFN was incorporated in the State of Illinois and operating as a not-for-profit organization.

Our Future

To date, the Caribbean-American Family Network’s signature event – the Annual Family Picnic – has seen attendance of over 400 individuals. Additionally, their other initiatives including in-home prayer meetings, crisis support, and partnerships with local missions have continued to grow in impact.

What We Do

Faith. Family. Community. Culture. Outreach.

As a network, we act as a catalyst for families to fully participate on all levels in the CAFN experience. As we grow, we envision ourselves as an organization that will play a critical role alongside families and communities at large. The Caribbean-American Family Network will continue to provide the following initiatives, and many more…

Outreach & Missions

In response to varying needs, CAFN hosts donation drives to collect books, toys, school supplies, children’s shoes, and basic medical supplies and partners with local and international Church Missions to send supplies to various Caribbean Islands in need. These items have been well received and truly fill a critical void.

Cultural Development

Members act as a resource for students at local colleges by hosting students in their homes, speaking on campuses, and providing cross-cultural Orientation Workshops. Our expertise includes executive life coaching, vocational discernment, helping you unleash your potential.

Business Development

We are convinced that as we educate, inform, and empower, we help you realize your calling, and develop your leadership skills. Walk boldly in your God-given purpose with CAFN + PennaSis leadership development workshops, coaching, speaking engagements, and 360* leadership assessments.

Resources & Referrals

The CAFN meets the needs of Sunday Schools, orphanages, churches, and local-neighborhoods in the Caribbean Islands by distributing ministry material that are desperately needed to spread the name of Jesus.

Spiritual Support

As a faith-based organization, CAFN makes every effort to ensure that its activities are Christ-centered. In addition, CAFN hosts worship services, provides in-home prayer meetings & crisis support for families both in and out of the network. CARN chaplains are always available and on call.

Fun & Fellowship Activities

Some of our events are intentional, and others are in true Caribbean fashion…spontaneous! A few of our evets include game nights, Christmas gatherings, our signature family picnic an family day, and worship services.
